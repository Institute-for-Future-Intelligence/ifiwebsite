Job Opening: Full Stack Software Engineering Internships

Posted on September 6, 2024

Compensation

$30-40 per hour

Description

The Institute for Future Intelligence is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization established in June, 2020 in Natick, Massachusetts. Our overarching mission is to search educational solutions in the era of artificial intelligence. We believe that a significant part of education would need to be revamped for future generations to adapt to the serious challenges posed by widespread use of artificial intelligence and embrace the great opportunities brought by it. Our goal is to become a leading institution of research and development that focuses on reimagining the relationship between human and machine and getting ready for its profound impacts on society.

We are seeking full stack software development interns to join a growing team to build innovative technological solutions for scientific research, engineering design, and STEM education, particularly in the fields of renewable energy, materials science, and drug discovery. A successful candidate will be an individual who has basic knowledge and experience in full stack technologies, takes pride and ownership in his/her work, and demonstrates strong passion for exploring and learning new things. There is a possibility that these internships will be converted into full-time positions funded for multiple years by several federal grants from the National Science Foundation and the National Institutes of Health to the Institute for Future Intelligence. Candidates must demonstrate ability to quickly learn Android/iOS/Web app development, cloud development, cyber-physical systems, and artificial intelligence. A solid foundation of mathematics, science, and engineering skills is also essential for these positions.

As a general policy, IFI sponsors OPT interns (and H-1B visas if needed).

Who you are

  • Dedicated to changing the world with innovative technologies
  • Meticulous about creating high-quality software at all levels
  • Interested in science, engineering, computation, and education
  • Excellent at communicating technical ideas to non-technical colleagues
  • Comfortable working in a team with members who have diverse backgrounds

What you will do

  • Develop Web-based, IoT-based, and cloud-based software solutions that serve students and teachers in distance learning
  • Develop software that support scientific research and engineering design with the assistance of generative AI
  • Collaborate with researchers and educators to create and improve products based on their feedback to meet release deadlines

What skills you should have

You are expected to have some combination of the following skills and qualifications:

  • HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript/TypeScript for creating Web apps
  • Java/Kotlin for Android or Swift for iOS or React Native for creating mobile apps
  • Backend frameworks such as Express.js or Flask for Python
  • Cloud APIs such as the Google Cloud Platform
  • Web frameworks such as React.js, Angular.js and Vue.js for creating Web apps and three.js for creating 3D interactives
  • Document databases such as MongoDB and Firebase
  • Physical computing with Raspberry Pi or Arduino
  • IDEs such as Android Studio and WebStorm
  • Ability to solve science and engineering problems with math
  • Bachelor's degree or higher in computer science or related fields

Interested?
